Biggest bang for the Security Buck: What kind of conversations do cybersecurity vendors need to have with the CISOs of large organizations?

By Dr. Yuri Bobbert

Over the last seven years, my role as Chief Information Security Officer (CISO) has drastically changed. According to my Microsoft Outlook Analytics client, more than fifty percent of my mail is spam from cybersecurity suppliers, trying to grab my attention by selling fear, uncertainty, and doubt.

Over the last decade, I've seen this industry explode with self-proclaimed "cyber pros" and fuzzy vendors in the cyber domain.

Research Methodology

This study examines the evolving role of CISOs and the challenges they face with cybersecurity vendors, based on seven years of experience in the field and analysis of vendor communication patterns.

Key Findings

  • Over 50% of CISO emails are vendor spam
  • Fear-based marketing tactics are prevalent
  • Industry has exploded with self-proclaimed experts
  • Need for better vendor-CISO communication
